US-MM6: feasibility of long-term proteasome
inhibition in multiple myeloma by in-class transition from bortezomib to ixazomib1

Study Design

US MM-6 is an ongoing US community–based, Real-world, open-label, single-arm,
phase 4 study in patients with newly diagnosed multiple myeloma who are transplant
ineligible or for whom transplant would be delayed ≥24 months and who are receiving
first-line VELCADE-based induction1
This study included Real-world patients who are often underrepresented in clinical trials because of eligibility criteria: 44% of patients were aged ≥75 years, 15% were Black or African American, 10% were Hispanic/Latino, 29% had creatinine clearance <60 mL/min, and 99% had any concurrent medical condition1
85% of patients received VRD, 13% received VCD, and 2% received other (VD, VR) induction regimens at the time of in-class transition to the Ixazomib (NINLARO) regimen1
The primary endpoint is 2-year PFS, from the first administration of the Ixazomib (NINLARO) regimen1

Patients receive 3 cycles of VELCADE-based induction (per regimens listed in NCCN guidelines)

Eligibility criteria:

  • ECOG and/or other performance status of 0 to 2
  • No grade ≥2 PN or grade 1 PN with pain
